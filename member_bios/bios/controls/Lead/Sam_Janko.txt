Samantha Janko is a Systems Engineering PhD student at Arizona State University Polytechnic. Coming from a background in Engineering Robotics (BS) in her undergraduate studies, she is experienced in system design, hardware development, and software controls. She is a current NSF GRFP fellowship recipient conducting research in the area of self-organizing  microgrids and next generation power distribution networks, while also participating in off-grid microgrid projects focused on bringing power to the developing world.As a part of the Hyperloop team, Sam is responsible for the design and development of the AZLoop competition pod power system. This includes consideration of power system sizing, modeling, component selection, power regulation, electrical infrastructure and safety circuits, power/battery management system controls, and integration of subsystems together.LinkedIn: www.linkedin.com/in/samantha-janko
